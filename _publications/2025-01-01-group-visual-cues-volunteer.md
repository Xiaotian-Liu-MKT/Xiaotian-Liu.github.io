### Abstract
This research explores how group visual cues in volunteer recruitment materials influence potential volunteers' intentions. Depicting volunteers in a group setting (vs. alone) can significantly boost recruitment effectiveness by activating "anticipated effervescence"â€”the expectation of collective joy, energy, and social connection. Across several studies, we demonstrate that this anticipated collective experience is a powerful motivator for prosocial behavior, particularly in community-based volunteering.

### Key Findings
1.  **Visual Framing**: Recruitment ads featuring groups of volunteers lead to higher click-through rates and sign-up intentions than those featuring a single volunteer.
2.  **Psychological Mechanism**: The effect is mediated by anticipated effervescence; potential volunteers are drawn to the promise of shared emotional energy.
3.  **Contextual Boundary**: The group effect is most prominent for events that are inherently social or community-focused.
